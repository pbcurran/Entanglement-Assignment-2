The Drip Selector

Project Background

Our website, The Drip Selector, gives users the ability to preview the look of items of clothing on a model. The app will allow users to select pictures of pre loaded clothes and see what they will look like together, for example a user will be able to select a t-shirt and a pair of jeans and the program will generate each of these on an avatar on the screen. This will allow the user to get an accurate representation of what the clothes will look like. Users will then be able to follow a link provided by the site which takes them to a store front that has the items of clothing they are previewing for sale. We will have different sections for Pants, t-shirts and other items of clothing to make the user experience more friendly and organized.

The clothes on the website will be clearly labeled so the user is quickly able to tell the brand and name of the clothes that they are previewing. The majority of the page will be taken up by the model that the clothes will be placed on and then next to this model will be the selection of clothes that the user can place on the model. Next to the pieces of clothing will be links that take the user to a storefront that sells that piece of clothing allowing them to quickly access a vendor for this piece.

Given the current situation Victoria is in, many people are not able to leave their suburbs, let alone purchase clothes and visit non-essential stores. Due to these restrictions that are in place, people are unable to receive a proper shopping experience which can lead to unexpected outcomes when online shopping, whether that be incorrect sizing or simply purchasing clothes that do not fit into their current wardrobe. We also quickly found that all members of the group share a common interest for clothing and trying new styles of clothing, as well as window shopping and browsing through various retailers and stores, prior to the current restrictions that are in place. As a result we decided to create a platform that would allow for a visual representation of what different pieces of clothing would look like together when worn in the same outfit, while also assisting online purchases in ensuring that the correct size of clothing is brought. In addition to this project being used as a tool for online shopping during COVID-19, individuals that are looking to expand their wardrobe and style would be able to see how different pieces of clothing would look like with each other before purchasing.

Aim and Goals

In line with our motivation, mentioned above, our aim for this project was to create a functioning webpage that will be able to offer a similar experience to shopping/browsing in store. With Victoria still currently under COVID-19 restrictions, shopping or browsing through clothes in physical locations is currently prohibited. This sparked the idea of creating a webpage that could work in place of physical stores whilst restrictions are still in place. With most of our group members currently studying subjects like Web Programming or Programming 1; as well as having some previous experience with coding experience, we all thought it would be a challenge to work together to create a functional website tailored to people like ourselves who would also benefit from its functionality.
Our first main goal was for our project idea to be approved by Dr B and our Mentor. With all members being relatively new to Web Programming and Programming, let alone collaborating with each other to create a fully functional website, we wanted to seek advice and information from a more experienced perspective as to how we should approach this project and what features will be required. After also presenting our idea to fellow peers and receiving their feedback, we were able to finalise what the main functionality of our final website will be as well as roughly divide the work and allocate roles based on individual strengths. Our second goal was to plan our approach to the project in our given situation. We also delegated each other roles based on our strengths and weaknesses, whilst also making it apparent as to who we should reach out to when in need for assistance, whether this be tutors or fellow group members. After laying out a plan to approach and go about the completion of this project, we each aimed to get familiar with the tools we were to be using for this project. More specifically, we ensured that each group member was familiar with how to use github to collaborate, as a few members were not experienced with this tool. Following all the preparation, our group also planned out our micro deadlines and meetings in order to keep the team and the production of the website on track.
Our third goal was to finalise our project itself. Knowing that external factors such as other assignments, extracurricular activities and roadblocks were more than likely to push us back in meeting micro deadlines; our group aimed to help each other out in this phase by providing any individual input/advice, working together in solving any problems and doing individual research and reporting any findings back to the group.

Purchasing Clothes From Partnered Stores

As said in previous features, the clothing that has been collected from different stores will also have an available link that will allow users to purchase from that selected store. This is done with the thought that users will want to purchase some of the items that have been used for demonstration purposes on the website.
This helps users be directly connected to a place to purchase making the user not have to search it to find it. There are two buttons: 'purchase' and 'view similar items' for each item on the website. The ‘purchase’ button will send you directly to the purchase side whereas the 'view similar items' will send you to the part of the website where similar items are available. For example, if you were to click on the 'view similar items' button under the tops selection, it would send you via the link where you can view more mens tops.

Extended Viable Features

Upon the successful implementation of our proposed MVFs, our group managed to plan how we would implement and begin these EVFs, should the website be continued in the future.

Sharing Outfits With Friends

An option to share outfits the user created on the website via other means of social media

User Stories

  • As a user, I have created a combination that I find interesting and wish to send this to other friends for their opinion. I want to be able to send this over social media without the hassle of manually doing it myself.
  • As a user, I want the ability to save the designs and share it with my family and friends at a later date if I wish to do so.

Textual Explanation

This feature gives the user the ability to share their customisations with those they wish to without going through the hassle of trying to manually do it. This should be an automatic process that is done by the website; it should allow the user to view their creation before uploading it to a social media of their choosing. A feature like this would benefit greatly for the website as it would give a more immersive experience while giving users more reason to search through the clothes.

Validation Test

Clicking a 'share' button should convert the current outfit on the avatar into a link that can be shared with friends.

Side View

An option to look at the outfit that is displayed on the dummy on either side

User Stories

  • As a user, I want the ability to look at either side of the outfit so that I know how it fully looks on a body
  • As a user who is restricted from going shopping in physical stores and retailers due to COVID-19 restrictions, I want to be able to spend my free time playing around with different outfits in a more immersive way.

Textual Explanation

This features while, quite simple in concept, will give more freedom to the user when viewing angles on clothes that they could view if they were shopping in stores. This will allow for a more immersive experience for the user and also provide a more in depth look at the clothes they have chosen. This will result in allowing the user to have a good reference of what it will truly look like by giving them a 360 degree field of view.

Validation Test

Clicking the right side of the avatar should show the side profile of the avatar.

Multiple Avatars

Having multiple different avatars to choose from that vary of different body type

User Stories

  • As a user, I want the ability to choose an avatar that matches the same body proportions that I currently have so I can have an accurate look of what it might look like on me.
  • As a user, when I save this outfit design I also want the save to have the same avatar I chose before so I do not need to go through the hassle of changing it again.
  • As a user, I want to also try it on other body types to see how the outfit would look on different instances.

Textual Explanation

This feature allows the user to choose from different types of avatars that consist of different body proportions. This not only adds more customizability, but also adds an extra feeling of accuracy regarding how it would look like on a body size more accurate to the user. This can give the real life feeling of clothes shopping while being at home due to the COVID-19 lockdown.

Validation Test

Multiple avatars will be shown with the aim to depict as many body sizes/shapes possible.

Future Improvements for our website

Given the small scale of this project being more a prototype than a final, fully operational website; we have chosen to hard code most elements, such as our images of clothing. This was done to ensure that our main intended functionalities of our website were to be completed and running smoothly.
In order to enhance our existing prototype and elevate it to a level where it is fit for real-world situations; future developments could include developing a more sophisticated back-end system whether this is in the form of a database or JSON file. This development will allow backend users to have easier access when updating/editing clothes and links, while also being able to accommodate our proposed extended features, such as saving multiple clothes under an account name and sharing them via social media platforms. These improvements will allow for our prototype to be used by a wider audience form customers to back end users.
Moving forward an area for improvement on the website could be creating a more realistic model for the clothes do be displayed on; this could be something like a virtual 3D model of a male or female that would make displaying the clothes for the user a more realistic and clear experience.
Relating back to our main motivations and purpose of our website, partnering up with local retailers, offering them a platform to showcase their range of products in a different way would also be a highly likely pathway for our project to go down.
